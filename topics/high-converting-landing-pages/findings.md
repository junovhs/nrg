# Findings — High-Converting Landing Pages for Indie Software

Source report (verbatim): [`working/landing-page-design-research.md`](working/landing-page-design-research.md), mined from the `ishoo` repo.

## Default to a dual-path page, not one message and not two sites
- **What:** For a product that must win both individuals and enterprise teams, the highest-converting default is a dual-path page: one obvious self-serve path for individuals/small teams, and one clearly available enterprise path (demos, security validation, procurement, custom pricing). B2B buyers want both self-service and human-assisted options, often in the same journey.
- **Evidence / source:** [`working/landing-page-design-research.md`](working/landing-page-design-research.md)
- **Confidence:** high
- **Date:** 2026-06-26

## The first screen must answer five questions fast
- **What:** Above the fold, answer: what the product is, who it's for, what outcome it creates, why it's credible, and what to do next. Users attend disproportionately to the top of the page, scan rather than read, and decide relevance within seconds.
- **Evidence / source:** [`working/landing-page-design-research.md`](working/landing-page-design-research.md)
- **Confidence:** high
- **Date:** 2026-06-26

## Use informative imagery, not stock art or carousels
- **What:** The hero should use real screenshots or a concise product demo — not generic stock imagery or rotating carousels.
- **Evidence / source:** [`working/landing-page-design-research.md`](working/landing-page-design-research.md)
- **Confidence:** high
- **Date:** 2026-06-26
