# Findings — Auto-Prioritized To-Do App Market Demand

Source report (verbatim): [`working/auto-prioritized-todo-market-research.md`](working/auto-prioritized-todo-market-research.md), mined from the `noceremony` repo.

## Demand for automatic prioritization is proven, but the market is segmented
- **What:** Real, already-proven demand exists for "tell me the best thing to do next," shown by AI schedulers (Motion, Reclaim, SkedPal, Sunsama) selling auto-ordering at meaningful price points and traction. But the need is not universal — task-management preferences differ by personality and job context.
- **Evidence / source:** [`working/auto-prioritized-todo-market-research.md`](working/auto-prioritized-todo-market-research.md)
- **Confidence:** high
- **Date:** 2026-06-26

## The job-to-be-done is validated; the specific KPI formula is not yet
- **What:** What's proven is the broader job (reduce decision fatigue, avoid missed deadlines, confidence the current order is sensible) and willingness to pay — not this product's exact formula.
- **Evidence / source:** [`working/auto-prioritized-todo-market-research.md`](working/auto-prioritized-todo-market-research.md)
- **Confidence:** medium
- **Date:** 2026-06-26

## Baseline market is large and fragmented
- **What:** Todoist (50M+ professionals, 30M+ downloads), Any.do (40M+), TickTick (10M+ downloads). A 2024 CHIWORK study found knowledge workers use an average of 8.9 task-management tools/methods — pain is real but users assemble patchwork systems.
- **Evidence / source:** [`working/auto-prioritized-todo-market-research.md`](working/auto-prioritized-todo-market-research.md)
- **Confidence:** high
- **Date:** 2026-06-26
